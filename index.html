Riddles!

Can you solve these riddles?

1. If you scare me, I might secrete poison from my spine.

2. You might have seen me on the big screen. I can assure you that the tank I’m in is clean

3. Please don’t step on me… despite my name, I am not a shoe.

4. You might have to look down to find me.

5. I can’t see you, but you can see me.

6. You might have to use your brain to find this piece of coral.

Fish Inventory

Horse Shoe Crab

These animals have inhabited earth for around 445 millions years. It uses its spiked tail to help flip itself over if they end up on their back.

DogFish Shark

Dogfish are usually harmless, but a poison can be secreted at the base of the spine occasionally in defense. They are the most common shark alive.

Royal Gramma

This fish is best known as Gurgle, the nervous and germophobic tank mate in the Disney Pixar film "Finding Nemo". It gets its name from its bright “royal purple” color.

Pork Fish

This fish is a species of Grunt that is native to the western Atlantic. It is normally nocturnal and can grow up to 12 inches.

Blue Chromis

This tiny fish can grow up to 3 inches in length on average. In fact, the largest blue chromis is only 3.1 inches in length. They mainly feed on phytoplankton.

Scorpion Fish

Scorpion fish are inactive during the day, and hunt at night. They have highly venomous spines that are used to deter predators.

Puffer Fish

These fish do not have scales and instead has thick and rough skin. Some have spines that offer protection against predators. They have four teeth that form a beak-like structure.

Slipper Lobster

Slipper lobsters are not considered ture lobsters because of their lack of claws. They are found in depths of 500 meters, where they use camouflage to hide from predators.

Brain Coral

This coral can live for hundreds of years, with some reaching 900 years old. They are considered a threatened species due to beach pollution.

Spotted Spiny Lobster

This lobster is also known as the guinea chick lobster. It has a lifespan of about 15 years and can grow five to eight inches long.

Horse Conch

This conch is the state shell of florida. It's the largest snail found in american waters. It's diet consists of clams, mussles, and other snails.

Lookdown fish

This fish is known for its thin and bright silvery-blue scales. It's diet consists of meaty foods. These fish are found in tropical and subtropical waters.

Blind Eye Cave Fish

These fish are known for having no eyes or pigment. They are also known as the mexican tetra and can grow up to 12 cm.

Slippery Dick

Slippery dicks are found in tropical and subtropical waters. They feed on invertebrates and its colors vary as they grow.

Caribbean Spiny Lobster

These lobsters tend to live in crevices of rocks and coral reefs. They can live up to around 100 years. The females carry their eggs underneath their tail.
